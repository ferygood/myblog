Notes from Statquest Fundamental statistics 01-07

Q: What is distribution for?

A: They show us how the probabilities of measurements are distributed.

Normal distribution: 95% of data are laid around the mean within +/-2std and it is symmetric based on the mean. (1) The average measurement. This tells you where the center of the curve is. (2) The standard deviation of the measurements, this tells you how wide the curve should be.

\(P(x) = \frac{1}{{\sigma \sqrt {2\pi } }}e^{{{ - \left( {x - \mu } \right)^2 } \mathord{\left/ {\vphantom {{ - \left( {x - \mu } \right)^2 } {2\sigma ^2 }}} \right. \kern-\nulldelimiterspace} {2\sigma ^2 }}} \)

Model: (1) We use model to explore relationship. (2) We use statistical test to determine how useful or how reliable our model is.

In statistics, we estimate the population mean and population variance/standard deviation from the relatively small number of measurements that we have. Use sample deviation to estimate population standard deviation $$\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} \left(x_{i} - \bar{x}\right)^{2}} {n-1}}$$

we use (n-1) to compensate the underestimate of population parameters.
